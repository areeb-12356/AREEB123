About Origami

Origami originated from China and was popularized in Japan. It is the art of transforming a simple sheet of paper into beautiful designs such as animals, birds, flowers and faces without cutting or pasting.

Origami Crane

Instruction For Origami Crane

What you need

square sheet of paper (any size; thin paper works best)

1. Start with a square:

Place the paper colored side down (if it has one).

2. Make diagonal folds:

Fold the paper in half diagonally (corner to corner).Unfold Fold diagonally the other way. Unfold again. You should see an X crease.

3. Fold in half horizontally and vertically:

Fold the paper in half (top to bottom), then unfold. Fold it in half again (left to right), then unfold. Now you have 8 crease lines.

4. Form a square base:

Hold the paper and gently push the sides inward. The paper will collapse into a smaller square (called a square base). Make sure the open ends face you.

5. Make a kite shape:

Take the top layer only. Fold the left edge to the center line. Fold the right edge to the center line. Fold the top triangle down. Unfold all three folds.

6. Make the inside reverse fold:

Lift the top flap. Open it and fold it upward, using the creases you just made. Flatten it into a long diamond shape. Turn the model over and repeat Step 5 and 6 on the other side.

7. Fold the neck and tail:

Take one of the long thin points. Fold it upward diagonally. Repeat with the other thin point.

8. Make the head:

Choose one thin point to be the head. Fold the tip down slightly to form the beak.

9. Fold down the:

wings Hold the body. Fold each large flap (wing) downward.

10. Gently pull :

the wings apart Your origami crane is complete üéâ

Origami Flower

Instruction For Origami Flower

What you need

square sheet of paper:

(Any size; colored paper looks best)

1. Start with a square:

Place the paper colored side down.

2. Fold diagonally:

Fold the paper corner to corner to make a triangle. Crease well.

Fold the triangle in half to make a smaller triangle.

Fold the bottom point upward to the top. Crease well.

7. Turn over again:

Flip it back to the front.

8. Open the flower:

Gently pull the petals apart. Flatten the center slightly. üéâ Your easy origami flower is finished!

Origami Butterfly

Instruction For Origami Butterfly

What you need

1. Start with the color side down:

Place the paper on a flat surface with the color side facing down.

2. Fold in half diagonally:

Fold the paper corner to corner to make a triangle. Crease well, then unfold.

3. Fold the other diagonal:

Fold along the other diagonal to make another triangle. Crease and unfold again.

4. Fold in half horizontally:

Fold the paper top to bottom (edge to edge). Crease and unfold.

5. Fold in half vertically:

Fold left to right, crease, then unfold. You should now see several crease lines forming a star or grid.

6. Collapse into a triangle base:

Gently push the left and right sides inward The paper will naturally collapse into a flat triangle (called a waterbomb base)

7. Fold the bottom corners up:

Take the two bottom corners of the triangle and fold them up to the top point.

8. Flip the paper over:

Turn the model over carefully.

9. Fold the top point down:

Fold the top triangle point down, letting it slightly extend past the bottom edge.

10. Fold in half:

Fold the butterfly in half vertically, wings outward.

11. Shape the wings

Gently pull the wings apart and adjust their shape with your fingers. üéâ Finished! You now have a cute origami butterfly!

Origami Box

Instruction For Origami Box

What you need

1. Start with the color side down:

Place the paper flat on the table with the colored side facing down.

2. Fold in half:

Fold the paper top to bottom to make a rectangle. Crease well and unfold.

3. Fold in half the other way:

Fold left to right to make another rectangle. Crease and unfold again. You should see a cross in the center.

4. Fold corners to the center:

Fold all four corners inward so they meet at the center point.

5. Fold top and bottom edges to the center:

6. Unfold the top and bottom edges:

Keep the corners folded, but unfold the top and bottom edges you just made.

7. Open the box sides:

Lift the left and right sides up while gently pushing the corners inward. The box will begin to form.

8. Shape the box:

Press the sides firmly and adjust the corners so the box stands neatly. üéâ Finished! You've made an easy origami box!

Origami Areoplane

Instruction For Origami Areoplane

What you need

1 rectangular sheet of paper (A4 or Letter):

1. Fold in half lengthwise:

Fold the paper in half from top to bottom. Crease well, then unfold.

2. Fold the top corners in:

Take the top left and top right corners and fold them inward so they meet the center crease. It should look like a triangle at the top.

3. Fold the new top edges in again:

Fold the slanted edges in toward the center crease one more time to make a sharp nose.

4. Fold the plane in half:

Fold the whole paper along the original center crease, with the folded edges on the inside. Make the wings On one side, fold the top layer down to form a wing. Flip over and repeat on the other side. Try to keep both wings the same size. Adjust and fly! Gently open the wings, hold the plane under the wings, and give it a smooth throw üöÄ üéâ Finished! You've made an easy origami Areoplane!

Origami Boat

Instruction For Origami boat

What you need
1 sheet of rectangular paper (A4 size works best)

1. Fold in Half:

Hold the paper horizontally (wide side towards you). Fold it top to bottom so the top edge meets the bottom edge. Press the fold gently to crease.

2. Fold in Half Again:

Now fold the paper left to right. Press to crease, then open it back. You will see a center line ‚Äî this helps alignment.

3. Make the Triangle Top:

Fold the top-left corner down toward the center line. Fold the top-right corner down toward the center line. Now the top looks like a triangle, and there are flaps at the bottom.

4. Fold the Bottom Flaps Up:

Take the front bottom flap and fold it up. Flip the paper over. Fold the other bottom flap up.

5. Open into a Square:

Hold the boat from the open part at the bottom. Gently pull apart to open the shape. Press the corners to flatten into a square.

6. Fold Bottom Flaps Again:

You now have a diamond/square shape. Fold the bottom point up to the top. Flip over and fold the other bottom point to the top.

7. Final Opening:

Hold the bottom open edges. Gently pull apart and open again. üéâ Finished! You've made an easy origami Boat!

Kusudama balls

Instruction For Origami Kusudama Balls

What you need
Square paper sheets (6 cm-10 cm size works well) 60 sheets total (5 petals X 12 flowers)

1. Start with a Square:

Place 1 square paper flat on the table.

2. Fold Diagonal:

Fold it corner to corner to make a triangle. Press crease gently.

3. Fold Corners Up:

Take the right corner of the triangle and fold it up to the top point. Take the left corner and fold it to the top point. Now it looks like a diamond / house shape.

4. Fold Flaps Outward:

Open the two flaps slightly and press them flat outward. They become kite shapes on each side.

5. Fold Tips Down:

On each kite, fold the small top tips downward so they align with the edges.

6. Fold Wings In:

Fold each side inward along the original creases. Now you will see a petal shape forming.

7. Close the Petal:

Apply a little glue on one inner wing edge. Bring the two sides together and hold for a few seconds. ‚Üí One petal is complete!

8. Make 5 Petals:

Repeat Part A until you have 5 petals.

9. Join Petals Together:

Put glue on the side of one petal. Attach a second petal to it. Continue until 5 petals form a full flower. ‚Üí One kusudama flower is now done.

10. Make 12 Flowers

Repeat the flower process until you have 12 flowers.

11. Join Flowers Slowly:

Add glue to touching petals. Join flowers one by one. Shape them into a round ball as you connect.

12. Final Adjustments:

Press gently so it holds shape. Let the ball dry for 10-20 minutes. üéâ Finished! You've made an easy origami Kusudama balls!

Square twist

Instruction For Origami Square twist

What you need
1 square sheet of paper (size 15x15 cm works well) A flat table No tools required

1. Start with a Square:

Place the paper color side down (if colored).

2. Fold in Half (Both Directions):

Fold left to right, crease, open. Fold top to bottom, crease, open. This creates a ‚Äú+‚Äù shape crease.

3. Fold Diagonally:

Fold corner to corner, crease, open. Fold opposite corners, crease, open. Now you have a ‚ÄúX‚Äù and ‚Äú+‚Äù combined.

4. Mark the Center Square:

Fold each side edge gently to the center crease (do NOT crease hard). Leave a small square in the center with light marks. This will guide the twist.

5. Make the Central Square Crease:

Using the light marks, fold a small square in the center by: Folding top edge toward center Folding bottom edge toward center Folding left edge toward center Folding right edge toward center Press gently so the center square is clear.

6. Create Triangle Corners:

From each side of the center square, fold a triangle inward: Pull one corner of the big square toward the nearest corner of the center square Press the fold lightly Repeat for all 4 corners Now you will see four triangle shapes around the small square.

7. Collapse the Twist:

Now we push it into shape: Hold the paper flat. Lightly pinch the four triangles at their tips. Gently push inward toward the center. The center square will sink down and twist by 90 degrees. Take your time‚Äîthere's no rush.

8. Flatten the Model:

Press firmly around the edges. Smooth the center square. üéâ Finished! You've made an easy origami Square twist!

Sculpted

Instruction For Origami Sculpted

What you need
1 square sheet of paper (10x10 cm or bigger) Your fingers for shaping (no tools needed)

1. Start With a Square:

Place the paper flat on the table.

2. Fold Diagonal:

Fold corner to corner to form a triangle. Crease gently.

3. Fold in Half Again:

Fold the triangle in half (small triangle). Crease lightly.

4. Open the Last Fold:

Open back to a bigger triangle. You now have one center guiding crease.

5. Make Accordion Folds:

Starting at one side, fold a small strip upward Flip and fold again (like a fan) Continue until the whole triangle is folded This creates the leaf veins look. Sculpting (Shaping the Paper) Now we do gentle shaping so the leaf looks natural.

6. Open the Fan Slightly:

Open the folds just a little so the leaf becomes wider.

7. Curve the Leaf:

Hold both ends and curve the leaf upward slightly. Use soft pressure, no sharp bends.

8. Shape the Tip:

Pinch the very tip gently for a pointed leaf look.

9. Add Natural Waves (Optional):

Using fingers: Press the edges gently to form small waves This gives a soft, sculpted appearance üéâ Finished! You've made an easy origami Sculpted!

Jumping frog

Instruction For Origami Jumping frog

What you need
1 rectangular sheet of paper (A4 size works well) Flat table No scissors or glue needed

1. Fold in Half Horizontally:

Place the paper landscape style (wide side toward you). Fold the top edge to the bottom edge. Crease and open. This creates a center crease.

2. Fold in Half Vertically:

Fold the left edge to the right edge. Crease and open. You will now have two creases intersecting at the center.

3. Make the Triangle Folds:

Fold the top-left corner down to the center crease. Fold the top-right corner down to the center crease. The top now forms a triangle shape.

4. Fold the Bottom Corners:

Take the bottom-left corner and fold it up to meet the triangle tip. Repeat with bottom-right corner. You now have a diamond shape.

5. Fold the Sides for Frog Legs:

Fold the left and right edges toward the center line. These will become the frog's back legs.

6. Fold the Frog in Half:

Fold the entire model backwards horizontally, so the legs are on the outside. Press gently to crease.

7. Make the Jumping Fold:

Fold the back corners outward to make the frog's hind legs. Press lightly to create small triangles pointing backward.

8. Make the Frog Jump:

Place the frog on a flat surface. Press down gently on the back and release ‚Äî it should jump forward! üéâ Finished! You've made an easy Jumping frog!

Bottle

Instruction For Origami Bottle

What you need
1 rectangular paper (A4 or letter size) Flat table Large paper makes folding easier for elders.

1. Fold in Half (Top to Bottom):

Place paper vertically (tall side up). Fold the top edge to the bottom edge. Press the crease and then open it back up. This gives you a center crease.

2. Fold Left & Right Edges to Center:

Take the left edge and fold it to the center crease. Take the right edge and fold it to the center crease. Now the paper looks like a door shape.

3. Form the Bottle Neck:

At the top part, fold both top corners inward slightly to form a narrower neck. This makes the top smaller than the body (bottle shape).

4. Fold the Shoulders of the Bottle:

Just below the neck, fold the left and right sides outward a little. This creates a rounded shoulder for the bottle.

5. Make the Bottle Bottom:

At the bottom, fold the edge upward about 1-2 cm. Press gently. This makes a flat base.

6. Shape the Neck (Optional):

For a nice neck shape: Fold a small part of the top edge backward. This creates a cap area. üéâ Finished! You've made an easy Bottle!

Bridge

Instruction For Origami Bridge

What you need
1 rectangular sheet of paper (A4 or Letter size) Flat table Large paper makes folding easy for elders.

1. Place Paper on the Table:

Put the paper horizontally (landscape) in front of you.

2. Fold Bottom Edge Up:

Take the bottom long edge and fold it up about 3-4 cm. Press gently to crease. This will become the bridge side wall.

3. Fold Top Edge Down:

Take the top long edge and fold it down about 3-4 cm. Press gently to crease. Now you have two folded side walls (these are the sides of the bridge).

4. Make the Bridge Road:

Now fold the entire paper in half, top to bottom. Press lightly, then open back up. This creates a middle guide crease.

5. Form the Bridge Arch:

Hold the paper at both ends. Gently push down in the center while lifting the sides. The paper will form a soft curved arch. No sharp folding here‚Äîjust gentle shaping with hands.

6. Stand the Bridge:

Place it on the table with: The folded strips as side supports The curved part as the bridge road If it sits unevenly, adjust the folds slightly. üéâ Finished! You've made an easy Bridge!

Samural Mask

Instruction For Origami Samural Mask

What you need
1 square sheet of paper (15x15 cm or larger) Flat surface Larger paper makes folding easier.

1. Start With a Square

Place the paper color side down (if there is color).

2. Fold in Half (Top to Bottom):

Fold the top edge down to the bottom edge. Press the crease gently. This forms a rectangle.

3. Fold in Half (Left to Right):

Fold the rectangle left to right. Press lightly, then open back up. This gives a center guide line.

4. Fold Top Corners Down:

Take the top-left corner and fold it toward the center line. Take the top-right corner and fold it toward the center line. Now the top looks like a triangle sitting on a rectangle.

5. Fold Bottom Corners Up:

Fold the bottom-left corner upward to meet the center. Repeat with the bottom-right corner. Now both top and bottom edges are tapered inward.

6. Fold Bottom Edge Up:

Fold the entire bottom edge up about 1-2 cm. This forms the chin area of the mask.

7. Fold Sides for Mask Cheeks:

Fold left side inward slightly (about 1 cm). Fold right side inward slightly. These create the cheek shapes of the samurai mask.

8. Form the Helmet Band:

At the top, fold down a small strip (about 1 cm). This creates the helmet band across the forehead.

9. Open Slightly & Adjust:

Gently open the mask just a little. Flatten the folds so the face shape shows clearly. üéâ Finished! You've made an easy Samural Mask !

Envelopes

Instruction For Origami Envelope

What you need
1 rectangular sheet of paper (A4 or Letter size works best) Flat table

1. Place Paper Vertically:

Put the paper in front of you so it is tall (portrait).

2. Fold Bottom Edge Up:

Fold the bottom long edge up to about two-thirds of the paper. Press gently to crease. (This will form the pocket of the envelope.)

3. Fold the Top Edge Down:

Fold the top long edge down until it overlaps the folded bottom section. Press the crease. (This creates the closing flap of the envelope.)

4. Fold Side Edges In:

Unfold everything gently. Now fold left side inward about 1-2 cm. Fold right side inward about 1-2 cm. (This makes the sides neat and helps the envelope close.)

5. Reform the Pocket:

Fold the bottom section up again along the same crease. Now you'll see a pocket space (envelope body).

6. Fold the Top Flap:

Fold the top part down again along the same crease. This becomes the seal flap of the envelope. üéâ Finished! You've made an easy Envelopes!

Rabbit

Instruction For Origami Rabbit

What you need
1 square sheet of paper (15x15 cm or bigger) Flat table Bigger paper makes folding easier.

1. Start With the Paper Colored Side Down:

If your paper has color, place the colored side facing the table.

2. Fold Into a Triangle:

Fold the square corner to corner to make a big triangle. Press slowly along the crease.

3. Fold Triangle in Half:

Take the triangle and fold it in half again (small triangle). Crease lightly, then open it back to the bigger triangle. This gives a center line.

4. Fold Bottom Corners Up:

Take the left corner of the triangle and fold it up toward the top corner. Take the right corner and fold it up toward the top corner. The shape now looks like a diamond. These are the rabbit's sides.

5. Flip the Paper Over:

Turn the whole shape over so the folds are facing down.

6. Fold the Left Corner to the Right:

Fold the left pointed corner over to the right pointed corner. Now you have a long shape, with a point at the top. This point will become the ears.

7. Fold Ears Up:

From the sharp top point, fold just the tip upward. This creates two ear points facing up. Flatten gently.

8. Make the Standing Base:

Fold the bottom pointed part slightly upward. This helps the rabbit stand.

9. Open Gently and Adjust:

Open the body slightly. Stand it on the bottom fold. Arrange the ears so they point upward. üéâ Finished! You've made an easy Rabbit!
